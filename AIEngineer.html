AI Engineer

INFO

Artificial intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that involves programming machines to think like human brains. AI engineering focuses on developing the tools, systems, and processes that enable artificial intelligence to be applied in the real world.

What Do AI Engineers Do?

  • Create and manage the AI development and production infrastructure
  • Conduct statistical analysis and interpret the results to guide and optimize the organization's decision-making process
  • Automate AI infrastructures for the data science team
  • Build AI models from scratch and help product managers and other stakeholders with analysis and implementation
  • Transform machine learning models into APIs that can be integrated with other applications

SKILLS

  • You'll want to learn programming languages such as Python, R, Java, and C++ so you can build and implement models.
  • These are needed to implement different AI and machine learning models.
  • AI engineers work with large amounts of data, so you'll be required to know Apache Spark, Hadoop, and MongoDB to manage it all.
  • You'll want to understand machine learning algorithms such as linear regression and Naive Bayes, as well as deep learning algorithms such as recurrent neural networks and generative adversarial networks, and be able to implement them with a framework. Common AI frameworks include Theano, TensorFlow, Caffe, Keras, and PyTorch.

SALARY

AI engineering is a specialized field that has promising job growth and tends to pay well. According to Glassdoor, the median base salary for an AI engineer is $101,991 in the United States
